About Me

I'm Jason Culbertson, a Senior Computer Science and Philosophy student at the University of Idaho with a focus on AI and Machine Learning. I got into CS at a young age, introduced to Scratch and Python in elementary school by my father who attended Boise State University for Computer Science.

During my time at U of I, I've had the chance to co-author a research paper on AI benchmarking, build a full video game with a team for my Software Engineering course, and dive deep into topics like adversarial robustness and data science. I try to spend time on projects that are both technically challenging and actually useful or interesting to real people.

Outside of school work, I like exploring new tools and frameworks, and the crossover between computer science and philosophy. I'm especially drawn to the harder questions in AI — not just can a model do something, but why, and what happens when it fails. I'm looking forward to bringing that mindset into my career after graduation.
